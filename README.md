# Income-Expectations-and-Portfolio-Choices
Link data on subjective income expectations to portfolio choices and to test whether individuals have private information about future realizations of earnings and whether individuals adjust their portfolio choices to these subjective expectations about future events.

The theory of portfolio choice relates asset shares to the expected value and riskiness of asset returns, transaction costs, and indivisibilities. However, portfolio decisions may also be affected by types of uncertainty other than interest-rate uncertainty. In particular, uninsurable, nondiversifiable risks like earnings risk may induce prudent investors to reduce holdings of risky assets in order to cut their overall exposure to risk.

The analysis will be based on several waves of the Survey of Household Income and Wealth, a large random sample representative of Italian households. The survey elicits information on the expected distribution of future earnings, and it contains detailed information about financial assets at the household level.
